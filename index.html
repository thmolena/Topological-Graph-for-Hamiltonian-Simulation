Lie-Topological Graph Policies for Hamiltonian Simulation

The order of terms in first-order Trotterization changes the simulation error at zero extra gate cost. We read that order off the commutator graph of the Hamiltonian and reach higher fidelity at a matched gate budget than random, coefficient- or locality-based orderings.

The problem

Digital quantum simulation approximates \(e^{-iHt}\) for \(H=\sum_j c_j P_j\) by a product of per-term rotations, repeated \(r\) times (Trotterization). The hardware cost is the gate count \(\propto |\text{terms}|\cdot r\); the accuracy is the Trotter error. Crucially, the error between two terms is set by their commutator \([P_a,P_b]\) — it is exactly zero when they commute — so the order in which the rotations are applied changes the error without changing the gate count.

Why a graph

Build a graph whose nodes are the Hamiltonian's terms and whose edges connect anticommuting pairs. Only edges contribute first-order Trotter error; the schedule is a walk on this graph.

Why ordering is free

Reordering the product leaves the gate proxy \(|\text{terms}|\cdot r\) untouched. The commutator ordering is a pure-software win: same circuit depth, lower error.

Why biology

Mapped molecular electronic-structure Hamiltonians are dense sums of local Pauli terms with irregular anticommutation — exactly the regime where ordering matters most.

Method

Five interchangeable orderings feed one first-order Trotter schedule; the reward is the exact fidelity against \(e^{-iHt}\) computed with scipy.linalg.expm.

1 · Pauli algebra
O(n) commutation test · exact rotation \(e^{-i\theta P}=\cos\theta\,I-i\sin\theta\,P\)
2 · Commutator graph
edge ⇔ anticommute · greedy commuting groups · invariant features
3 · Ordering policy
commutator / learned router → schedule minimizing weighted anticommuting adjacencies
4 · Audited reward

Why the demo is exact

Every Pauli string squares to the identity, \(P^2=I\), so it has eigenvalues \(\pm1\) and the single-term evolution has a closed form with no matrix exponential:

$$e^{-i\theta P}=\cos\theta\,I-i\sin\theta\,P,\qquad \big(e^{-i\theta P}\big)|\psi\rangle=\cos\theta\,|\psi\rangle-i\sin\theta\,(P|\psi\rangle).$$

Two terms commute iff the number of qubits on which their factors anticommute is even — an O(n) parity check, the commutator-graph edge rule. First-order Trotter error between two terms scales with \(|c_a||c_b|\,\lVert[P_a,P_b]\rVert\), which is zero when they commute; the commutator ordering greedily keeps adjacent terms commuting (or cheap when not).

The package ships this fast statevector rotation and a dense scipy.linalg.expm reference, and a test asserts they agree to \(10^{-9}\) on random Paulis — so the fast in-browser Trotter is the trusted math.
